# Sora Camera Movements Guide

## Why Camera Movement Matters

Camera movement adds depth, realism, and cinematic storytelling to AI-generated videos. Choosing the right movement helps create engaging and professional-looking scenes.

---

# Basic Camera Movements

## Static Shot
The camera remains fixed, keeping the subject centered.

## Dolly In
The camera smoothly moves toward the subject to create focus and emotion.

## Dolly Out
The camera moves away from the subject to reveal more of the environment.

## Pan Left
The camera rotates smoothly to the left while staying in the same position.

## Pan Right
The camera rotates smoothly to the right.

## Tilt Up
The camera moves upward to reveal tall subjects or dramatic scenery.

## Tilt Down
The camera moves downward to highlight objects below.

## Orbit Shot
The camera circles around the subject for a dynamic cinematic effect.

## Tracking Shot
The camera follows the subject while maintaining smooth movement.

## Crane Shot
The camera moves vertically to create epic establishing shots.

## Aerial Shot
A bird's-eye view showing the environment from above.

## Close-Up
Focuses on facial expressions or important details.

## Wide Shot
Shows the entire scene and surrounding environment.

A young explorer walking through an ancient jungle temple, cinematic tracking shot, soft morning sunlight, realistic atmosphere, ultra detailed, 4K.

---

# Best Practices

- Use smooth camera movements.
- Match movement to the story.
- Keep the main subject in focus.
- Avoid unnecessary camera changes.
- Combine lighting with camera motion.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Using too many camera movements.
- Fast or unrealistic motion.
- Conflicting camera directions.
- Ignoring subject consistency.
