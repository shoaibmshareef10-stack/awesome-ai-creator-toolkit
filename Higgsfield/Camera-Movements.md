# Higgsfield Camera Movements Guide

## Why Camera Movement Matters

Camera movement makes AI videos more cinematic, immersive, and realistic. Choosing the right movement improves storytelling and visual quality.

---

# Basic Camera Movements

## Static Shot
The camera remains completely still.

## Dolly In
The camera slowly moves toward the subject.

## Dolly Out
The camera slowly moves away from the subject.

## Pan Left
The camera rotates smoothly to the left.

## Pan Right
The camera rotates smoothly to the right.

## Tilt Up
The camera moves upward.

## Tilt Down
The camera moves downward.

## Orbit
The camera circles around the subject.

## Crane Shot
The camera moves vertically for dramatic scenes.

## Tracking Shot
The camera follows the subject naturally.

## Handheld
Adds realistic handheld camera motion.

---

# Example Prompt

A fearless explorer walking through an ancient jungle temple, slow dolly in, cinematic lighting, ultra realistic, smooth camera movement.

---

# Best Practices

- Use smooth camera motion.
- Match movement to the scene.
- Keep the subject in focus.
- Avoid unnecessary camera changes.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Using too many movements.
- Fast unrealistic motion.
- Conflicting camera directions.
- Ignoring subject consistency.
