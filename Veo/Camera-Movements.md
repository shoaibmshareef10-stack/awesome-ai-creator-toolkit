# Veo Camera Movements Guide

## Introduction

Camera movement is one of the most powerful storytelling tools in Veo. Smooth and realistic motion makes AI-generated videos feel cinematic and immersive.

---

# Why Camera Movement Matters

Good camera movement:
- Creates emotion
- Adds realism
- Directs viewer attention
- Makes scenes feel like real films

---

# Basic Camera Shots

## Static Shot
Camera remains fixed.

Best For:
- Dialogue
- Interviews
- Product videos

---

## Pan

Moves left or right.

Best For:
- Revealing environments
- Following subjects

---

## Tilt

Moves up or down.

Best For:
- Tall buildings
- Character reveals

---

## Zoom In

Moves closer to the subject.

Creates:
- Emotion
- Drama
- Focus

---

## Zoom Out

Moves away from the subject.

Creates:
- Scale
- Isolation
- Scene endings

---

# Cinematic Camera Movements

## Dolly In
Slow forward movement toward the subject.

## Dolly Out
Slow backward movement.

## Tracking Shot
Camera follows the subject naturally.

## Orbit Shot
Camera circles around the character.

## Crane Shot
Vertical cinematic movement.

## Drone Shot
Wide aerial view.

## Handheld
Natural documentary-style movement.

- 50mm – Cinematic portraits

# Example Prompt

Slow dolly-in camera, eye-level angle, 50mm cinematic lens, natural handheld stabilization, smooth motion, realistic focus pull, blockbuster composition.

---

# Pro Tips

- Keep movements smooth and natural.
- Avoid combining too many movements.
- Match movement to the emotion of the scene.
- Use tracking shots for action and dolly shots for drama.
- Specify the lens for more cinematic results.
