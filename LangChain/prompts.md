# 📝 Prompts

## Overview

Prompts are the instructions you give to a language model. Well-written prompts produce more accurate, relevant, and consistent responses.

---

## Why Prompts Matter

Good prompts help you:

- Improve response quality
- Reduce ambiguity
- Increase accuracy
- Save tokens
- Build reliable AI applications

---

## Types of Prompts

### Simple Prompts

Ask a direct question or request.

Example:

> Explain how LangChain works.

---

### Instruction Prompts

Clearly define what the model should do.

Example:

> Summarize this article in five bullet points.

---

### Contextual Prompts

Provide additional information before asking a question.

Example:

> You are an AI coding assistant. Explain this Python function.

---

### Structured Prompts

Specify the expected output format.

Example:

> Return the answer as a JSON object.

---

## Prompt Engineering Tips

- Be clear and specific.
- Provide enough context.
- Define the desired output format.
- Break large tasks into smaller prompts.
- Test different prompt variations.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Writing vague prompts.
- Providing insufficient context.
- Asking multiple unrelated questions at once.
- Ignoring response validation.

---

## Best Practices

- Keep prompts simple.
- Use examples when needed.
- Refine prompts based on results.
- Reuse successful prompt templates.
- Measure prompt performance.

---

## Summary

Effective prompts are the foundation of successful AI applications. Better prompts lead to more accurate, reliable, and useful responses.
