# Kling Camera Movements

## Introduction

Camera movement adds realism, emotion, and cinematic storytelling to AI-generated videos. Choosing the right movement can dramatically improve the final result.

---

# Basic Camera Movements

## Static Shot
The camera remains completely still.

## Pan Left
The camera smoothly moves left.

## Pan Right
The camera smoothly moves right.

## Tilt Up
The camera moves upward.

## Tilt Down
The camera moves downward.

## Dolly In
The camera slowly moves toward the subject.

## Dolly Out
The camera slowly moves away from the subject.

## Tracking Shot
The camera follows the subject while moving.

## Orbit Shot
The camera circles around the subject.

## Crane Shot
The camera moves vertically for dramatic cinematic scenes.

---

# Best Camera Movements

- Portrait → Dolly In
- Action → Tracking Shot
- Landscape → Drone Shot
- Emotional Scene → Slow Dolly In
- Product Video → Orbit Shot

---

# Example Prompt

A confident superhero walking through a futuristic city, cinematic lighting, tracking shot, smooth motion, ultra realistic.

---

# Best Practices

- Keep camera movement smooth.
- Use only one primary movement per scene.
- Match movement with the emotion.
- Avoid excessive camera motion.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Random camera movements.
- Multiple movements in one short clip.
- Fast motion without purpose.
- Ignoring subject framing.
