# Flux Lighting Guide

## Introduction

Lighting is one of the most important elements in AI image generation. It controls realism, mood, depth, and visual quality. The right lighting can transform an ordinary image into a cinematic masterpiece.

---

# Popular Lighting Styles

## Cinematic Lighting

High contrast lighting inspired by movies with dramatic highlights and shadows.

---

## Golden Hour

Warm sunlight during sunrise or sunset, perfect for portraits and landscapes.

---

## Blue Hour

Cool blue lighting shortly after sunset, creating a calm and dreamy atmosphere.

---

## Studio Lighting

Professional lighting setup for portraits and product photography.

---

## Soft Lighting

Even, diffused light with gentle shadows for a natural look.

---

## Hard Lighting

Strong directional light that creates bold shadows and dramatic contrast.

---

## Volumetric Lighting

Visible light rays passing through fog, smoke, or windows for cinematic depth.

---

## Rim Lighting

A bright outline around the subject that separates it from the background.

---

## Neon Lighting

Colorful glowing lights, ideal for cyberpunk and futuristic scenes.

---

## Moonlight

Cool blue night lighting that creates a peaceful and realistic nighttime mood.

---

# Best Lighting for Different Subjects

| Subject | Recommended Lighting |
|----------|----------------------|
| Portrait | Soft Lighting |
| Product | Studio Lighting |
| Landscape | Golden Hour |
| Action Scene | Cinematic Lighting |
| Fantasy | Volumetric Lighting |
| Cyberpunk | Neon Lighting |

---

# Example Prompt

A futuristic robot standing in a rainy neon city, cinematic lighting, volumetric light rays, ultra realistic, highly detailed, 8K.

---

# Best Practices

- Match lighting with the scene.
- Keep the light direction consistent.
- Use realistic shadows.
- Avoid combining too many lighting styles.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Flat lighting.
- Missing lighting description.
- Unrealistic shadows.
- Conflicting lighting effects.
