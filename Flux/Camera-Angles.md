# Flux Camera Angles Guide

## Introduction

Camera angles help create depth, emotion, and storytelling in AI-generated images. Choosing the right angle can dramatically improve the final result.

---

# Eye Level

Natural perspective for portraits and everyday scenes.

---

# Close-up

Highlights facial expressions and fine details.

---

# Medium Shot

Shows the subject from the waist up while keeping the background visible.

---

# Wide Shot

Captures the full subject along with the surrounding environment.

---

# Low Angle

Makes the subject appear powerful, confident, and heroic.

---

# High Angle

Makes the subject appear smaller, weaker, or more vulnerable.

---

# Bird's Eye View

A top-down perspective ideal for landscapes, cities, and large scenes.

---

# Worm's Eye View

An extreme low-angle view that creates a dramatic and cinematic effect.

---

# Over-the-Shoulder

Useful for conversations, action scenes, and storytelling.

---

# Dutch Angle

A tilted camera angle used to create tension, suspense, or unease.

---

# Example Prompt

A futuristic soldier standing on a rooftop, low-angle shot, cinematic lighting, dramatic clouds, ultra realistic, highly detailed.

---

# Best Practices

- Match the angle to the story.
- Keep the subject clearly visible.
- Use wide shots for landscapes.
- Use close-ups for emotions.
- Use low angles for heroic characters.

---

# Common Mistakes

- Random camera angles.
- Poor framing.
- Cropped subjects.
- Inconsistent perspective.
