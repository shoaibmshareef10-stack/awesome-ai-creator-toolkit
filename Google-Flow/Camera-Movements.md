# Camera Movements Guide

## Introduction

Camera movement adds emotion, realism, and cinematic storytelling to AI-generated videos. Choosing the right movement can dramatically improve the final result.

---

# Basic Camera Movements

## Static Shot
The camera does not move.

Best For:
- Interviews
- Dialogue
- Product shots

---

## Pan Left / Right

The camera rotates horizontally.

Best For:
- Revealing a location
- Following a subject

---

## Tilt Up / Down

The camera rotates vertically.

Best For:
- Showing height
- Dramatic reveals

---

## Zoom In

Moves closer to the subject.

Best For:
- Emotional moments
- Important details

---

## Zoom Out

Moves away from the subject.

Best For:
- Scene endings
- Environment reveals

---

## Dolly In

The camera physically moves toward the subject.

Creates:
- Tension
- Drama

---

## Dolly Out

Camera moves backward.

Creates:
- Isolation
- Sadness

---

## Tracking Shot

Camera follows the character.

Perfect For:
- Walking
- Running
- Action scenes

---

## Orbit Shot

Camera circles around the subject.

Perfect For:
- Hero introductions
- Cinematic reveals

---

## Crane Shot

Camera moves vertically.

Best For:
- Epic endings
- Large environments

---

## Drone Shot

Aerial cinematic view.

Perfect For:
- Cities
- Mountains
- Establishing shots

---

# Camera Speed

- Slow
- Smooth
- Natural
- Fast
- Cinematic

---

# Pro Tips

- Never combine too many camera movements.
- Use smooth motion.
- Match movement with emotion.
- Use cinematic pacing.
- Keep movement realistic.
