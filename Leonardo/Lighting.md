# Leonardo Lighting Guide

## Introduction

Lighting is one of the most important elements in AI image generation. It controls realism, mood, depth, and cinematic quality.

---

# Popular Lighting Styles

## Cinematic Lighting

Perfect for movie-quality images with dramatic contrast.

## Golden Hour

Warm sunlight during sunrise or sunset.

## Blue Hour

Soft blue lighting after sunset.

## Studio Lighting

Professional lighting for portraits and product photography.

## Soft Lighting

Natural, diffused light with gentle shadows.

## Hard Lighting

Strong contrast with sharp shadows.

## Volumetric Lighting

Visible light rays passing through fog, smoke, or windows.

## Rim Lighting

Creates a glowing outline around the subject.

## Neon Lighting

Ideal for cyberpunk and futuristic artwork.

## Moonlight

Cool blue lighting for realistic night scenes.

---

# Matching Lighting with Subjects

| Subject | Best Lighting |
|----------|---------------|
| Portrait | Soft Lighting |
| Product | Studio Lighting |
| Landscape | Golden Hour |
| Action | Cinematic Lighting |
| Fantasy | Volumetric Lighting |
| Cyberpunk | Neon Lighting |

---

# Example Prompt

A futuristic warrior standing in a neon city, cinematic lighting, volumetric light rays, ultra realistic, highly detailed.

---

# Best Practices

- Match lighting with the mood.
- Keep the light direction consistent.
- Use natural shadows.
- Avoid mixing too many lighting styles.

---

# Common Mistakes

- No lighting description.
- Flat lighting.
- Unrealistic shadows.
- Conflicting lighting styles.

---

# Pro Tips

- Use Golden Hour for warm scenes.
- Use Volumetric Lighting for cinematic depth.
- Use Studio Lighting for products.
- Use Neon Lighting for futuristic artwork.
